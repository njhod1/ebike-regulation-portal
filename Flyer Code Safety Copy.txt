\section*{1. THE COMPLIANCE STICKER RULE}
Under the \textbf{Road Rules 2014}, any e-bike used in public must be an EPAC (Electrically Power Assisted Cycle).
\begin{itemize}[label=--, nosep, topsep=4pt]
    \item \textbf{The 250W Cap:} Motor power must not exceed 250W. The previous 500W allowance ended Dec 2025.
    \item \textbf{Mandatory Labeling:} A permanent \textbf{EN 15194 compliance sticker} must be visible on the frame.
    \item \textbf{Rating > 250W:} Legally classified as a \textbf{Motorcycle}. Riding without registration, insurance, and a license is a criminal offense subject to vehicle impoundment.
\end{itemize}

\section*{2. SEIZURE \& RETAILER ACCOUNTABILITY}
Pursuant to the \textbf{Road Transport Act 2013}, Police are conducting active roadside inspections:
\begin{itemize}[label=--, nosep, topsep=4pt]
    \item \textbf{No Sticker = Immediate Seizure:} Bikes lacking compliance stickers are presumed illegal.
    \item \textbf{Retailer Warning:} Businesses selling motorbikes as "e-bikes" without road-legality warnings are in breach of \textbf{Australian Consumer Law}. Report illegal sales to Fair Trading.
    \item \textbf{Dyno Testing:} Police use portable Dyno units to test motor wattage output in real-time.
\end{itemize}

\section*{3. FEDERAL IMPORT \& OFF-ROAD USE}
The \textbf{Road Vehicle Standards Act 2018} now restricts the entry of high-powered "e-motorbikes." 
\begin{itemize}[label=--, nosep, topsep=4pt]
    \item \textbf{Off-Road Only:} Motors over 250W are strictly for \textbf{Private Property Only}.
    \item \textbf{The "Gap" Period:} Bikes imported 2021--2025 are not "grandfathered." If they exceed 250W, they are illegal for public road use today.
\end{itemize}
